HEADER

As with most other headers, a company brand logo is included and is usually located in the upper left part of the header. As you can see by my example the layout is flexible, in this case the company brand is located in the center of the header. Users are able to access different parts of the site using links that are also located in the header as well. These additional links are typically located to the right of the logo in the case of my example header the additional links are locate beneath.


Content Visualization:

Headers are the most frequent location for global navigation links that span the site. The ideal arrangement is to use an html list of links, styled with css to spread horizontally across a section of the header

CALL TO ACTION

A CTA can be a simple non-demanding request like "choose a color" or "watch this video", or a much more demanding request like "Buy Now!". Typical CTA work by requesting the audience to purchase a product or provide personal details and contact information.

Content Visualization:

This call to action is a fairly simple one consisting of some text and an image. Typically call to Actions also include a link or button.

Blog

A blog is a discussion forum where you can make posts and people can post comments and others can post replies to those comments. One of the benefits of a blog is that it keeps people reading your material. A disadvantage is that you have to make relevant posts and also monitor the responses.

Content Visualization:

There are many ways that a blog can b developed, you can make scrolling testimonial box (something like a slideshow) by using a standard Gallery Block and nest an image with captions.

FOOTER

Footer

A footer is an area at the bottom of a document page that contains information, links etc. that is related to the content on the websites pages.

Content Visualization:

The footer is typically made up of a footer tag and the content within. The content that a footer is comprised of varies from website to website but typically has an "About Us" Link, Contact Us Link, the websites Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Site Map and the Contact Details. Developers will also use the footer area to include information about where the site was developed and who developed it.
